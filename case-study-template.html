Non-profit Homepage Redesign

Our Hypothesis: We believe that redesigning information hierarchy will allow users to better engage with our website through restructuring main navigation and content hierarchy on the homepage, thus, opening up new opportunities for users to get involved with the organization.

What We Learned: Redesigning the YMH Project homepage will also achieve a more meaningful, seamless experience that allows users to find the information that they are looking for quicker and with less frustration, especially for youth experiencing a mental health crisis.

Role: UX/UI Designer

Tools: Miro, Figma, InVision, Google Drive, Trello, Canva, Slack, Zoom

Governemnt Agency Homepage Redesign

The Problem: The USDA website allows users to access USDA information no matter where they are whether it’s an office, grocery store, or the middle of a farmer's field, and regardless of what device or browser they're using. The problem is the information that users are looking for is difficult to find.

The Solution: Redesigning the information hierarchy and simplifying the the content made it easier for users to navigate and asbord the content they were looking for. This also allowed users to find what they were looking for with less frustration.

Role: UX/UI Designer

Tools: Miro, Figma, InVision, Google Drive, Trello, Canva, Slack, Zoom

On Queue

The Problem: Waiting in line is sometimes inevitable and can be time consuming. Being late for an event can cause stress and anxiety. There currently isn’t a tool that allows people to see how long line wait times are.

The Solution: Create a tool that allows users to live track line wait times. This will allow users to better delegate their time before an event. Also, adding a feature that allows users to skip the line all together to combat the fear of missing out.

Role: UX/UI Designer

Tools: Slack, Zoom, Miro, Figma, InVision, Google Drive, Trello
